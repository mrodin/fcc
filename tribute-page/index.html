Søren Kierkegaard

Danish philosopher who is widely considered to be the first existentialist.

Here's a time line of Kierkegaard's life:

  • 1813 - Birth in Copenhagen at Nytorv 2, son of Michael Pedersen Kierkegaard and Anne Sørensdatter Lund Kierkegaard
  • 1821 - Enrolled in Borgerdydskolen in Copenhagen (School of Civic Virtue)
  • 1828 - Confirmed in Vor Frue Kirke by Pastor J. P. Mynster
  • 1830 - Entered the University of Copenhagen as a theology student
  • 1831 - Finishes first part of second examination (Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and history, magna cum laude; mathematics, summa cum laude)
  • 1834 - First entry into journals (entry I A 1)
  • 1835 - The so-called "Great Earthquake". Kierkegaard believes that a curse lay over the family when he discovers that his father had cursed God as a young man.
  • 1838 - Kierkegaard records that he has experienced "an indescribable joy" (see the Journals, II A 228).
  • 1840 - Completes examination for degree (magna cum laude)
  • 1841 - Kierkegaard leaves to study in Berlin
  • 1842 - Returns to Copenhagen
  • 1843 - Either/Or
  • 1845 - Three Discourses on Imagined Occasions
  • 1847 - Upbuilding Discourses in Various Spirits
  • 1848 - Kierkegaard writes in his Journals, "My whole nature is changed. My concealment and reserve are broken—I am free to speak" (VIII A 640).
  • 1850 - Kierkegaard moves to Nørregade 43, Copenhagen
  • 1852-54 - The so-called "Gathering Storm": Kierkegaard published nothing until December of 1854, when he began the attack on Official Christianity
  • 1855 - Death in Copenhagen

“Marry, and you will regret it; don’t marry, you will also regret it; marry or don’t marry, you will regret it either way. Laugh at the world’s foolishness, you will regret it; weep over it, you will regret that too; laugh at the world’s foolishness or weep over it, you will regret both. Believe a woman, you will regret it; believe her not, you will also regret it… Hang yourself, you will regret it; do not hang yourself, and you will regret that too; hang yourself or don’t hang yourself, you’ll regret it either way; whether you hang yourself or do not hang yourself, you will regret both. This, gentlemen, is the essence of all philosophy.”

Søren Kierkegaard
